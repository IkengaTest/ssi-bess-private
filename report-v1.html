The SSI Enhanced Neural Network

The SSI Index v4.0 scores 4,293 Italian distribution substations across six dimensions — Congestion (C), Voltage (V), Intermittency (I), Economic fabric (E), DER Saturation (S), and Transition trajectory (T) — answering a single question: how stressed is this substation?

The SSI Enhanced Neural Network (SSI-ENN) extends that measurement into a valuation platform. For every scored substation, it answers two further questions: what is a BESS worth at this location — to the distribution network operator, to the local community, and to the asset owner? And what is the real-option value of that investment over a 30-year horizon?

For CP Fiumetorto, the engine ingested 20 sub-metrics across 6 SSI components from 15 verified public data sources, applied 4 multiplicative modifiers (R3, R4, R6, R7) through the modifier chain, and propagated uncertainty through a 20×20 Gaussian copula correlation matrix (190 unique dependency pairs, including tail-dependent Clayton mixture for joint-extreme events). Three-stakeholder decomposition. Unlike single-NPV models, the SSI-ENN decomposes every BESS investment into three distinct value pools: VDSO (network services value to the distribution operator), VCommunity (monetised social welfare across five components W1–W5), and VRO (merchant revenue and option value to the asset owner). This separation is deliberate — different stakeholders face different discount rates, risk preferences, and time horizons. The DSO values congestion relief and resilience; the community values reliability, employment, and climate adaptation; the owner values IRR and Sharpe ratio. Aggregating these into a single number would obscure the investment thesis.

Strategic positioning. The SSI-ENN is not a battery sizing tool. This report evaluates the Config B reference specification (9.9 MW / 39.6 MWh), which sits below the 10 MW distribution connection threshold — ensuring the asset remains distribution-connected and maps directly onto the SSI’s substation-level architecture. The engine is designed to accommodate arbitrary storage specifications, demand-side assets, and other flexibility technologies (V2G, hydrogen electrolysers, community solar+storage).

BESS-driven congestion relief reduces effective industrial energy costs by 3–8%. Sicily experiences congestion rents of €15–40/MWh above the national PUN. Lower operating costs sustain existing employment and reduce business closure in energy-intensive sectors. Estimated 5–12 FTEs.

4. Energy Services Ecosystem

An operational BESS creates a local nucleus for energy-services firms: monitoring, predictive maintenance, power electronics, and EV charging. In provinces with utility-scale BESS, energy-services firms grow by 5–12% within three years (GSE, 2024). Estimated 5–15 FTEs.
