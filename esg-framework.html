SSI-ENN Technical Note

ESG Value Uplift — Quantification Framework

Defining VESG for the BESS Stakeholder Value Model: Regulatory Basis, Formula Construction, Auditable Data Sources & Vintage Requirements

Altinium Invest · Ikenga Infrastructure · Working Draft · March 2026

1   Problem Statement

The SSI-ENN BESS valuation model (Plan of Work v27) defines five DSO value components D1–D5, all of which measure avoided costs to the distribution network. The DSO value table in the v1 report carries a sixth row — ESG Value Uplift — marked “TBD” and annotated as “not in v27 D1–D5 framework — additional Altinium metric.”

This note answers four questions: (i) what regulatory and market-practice foundations exist to quantify an ESG value uplift for grid-connected BESS assets; (ii) what formula structure is defensible; (iii) what validates each component; and (iv) what auditable data sources, with what vintage, should feed the calculation.

Scope note: VESG is not a revenue stream to the investor. It is a socio-economic externality value that sits alongside VDSO and VCommunity in the Three-Value Framework. Its purpose is to inform policy dialogue, ESG-linked financing terms, and EU Taxonomy alignment reporting — not to enter the investor’s NPVbase.

2   Regulatory & Market-Practice Foundations

2.1   EU Taxonomy Regulation (2020/852)

The Taxonomy classifies energy storage under Climate Change Mitigation (Article 10.1(a): “generating, transmitting, storing, distributing or using renewable energy”). Technical Screening Criteria (Climate Delegated Act, Annex I, Activity 4.10) require demonstration that the storage facility enables integration of renewable energy or provides grid-balancing services that reduce fossil dispatch. A BESS that meets these criteria qualifies as Taxonomy-aligned, which is the gateway to the greenium channel (see §2.4).

Implication for VESG: Taxonomy alignment provides the legal basis for claiming avoided-emissions value. The Technical Screening Criteria effectively define the “eligibility test” — if the asset passes, its displaced fossil generation can be monetised at a shadow carbon price.

2.2   EU ETS & Shadow Carbon Pricing

The EU Emissions Trading System provides the market-observed carbon price. In 2025, ETS-1 allowances traded between €60–€80/tCO2, with a forward curve signalling ~€2 annual increases. For societal valuation (which captures damages beyond the market price), the EPA’s 2023 update set the Social Cost of Carbon (SCC) at $190/tCO2 (~€175) at a 2% discount rate, while the Stern-Stiglitz High-Level Commission recommended $50–$100/tCO2 by 2030, escalating thereafter.

For a conservative, auditable framework we use the EU ETS spot price as the floor and the EPA SCC (converted to EUR) as the ceiling, with the mid-point as the central estimate. This avoids policy-dependent assumptions while remaining grounded in observable markets.

2.3   Italy Grid Emission Factor (ISPRA)

ISPRA (Istituto Superiore per la Protezione e la Ricerca Ambientale) publishes annual emission factors for Italy’s electricity grid. The 2024 edition reports an average grid intensity of ~0.270 tCO2eq/MWh. However, for displaced-fossil calculations, the relevant metric is the marginal emission factor — the carbon intensity of the marginal plant that the BESS displaces when it injects stored energy. In Sicily/Southern Italy, the marginal plant is predominantly CCGT gas at ~0.37–0.42 tCO2/MWh.

2.4   Green Financing Premium (Greenium)

EU Taxonomy-aligned BESS assets access green bond markets. Empirical evidence from 2024–2025 shows a greenium of 3–15 bps on senior secured infrastructure debt in European markets (with advanced-economy averages ~2 bps compressing as supply grows, and infrastructure-specific deals achieving 8–15 bps). Beyond the debt greenium, ESG performance empirically reduces cost of equity by 10–30 bps and pre-tax WACC by ~1 bp per unit of environmental score improvement. Over a 30-year asset life with €7M CAPEX, even a 20 bps blended WACC reduction yields measurable NPV uplift.

2.5   ARERA & Italian DSO Framework

ARERA (Autorità di Regolazione per Energia Reti e Ambiente) does not yet assign explicit ESG premia in the regulated revenue formula for DSOs. However, ARERA’s mandate includes “environmental protection and efficient use of resources” (L. 481/1995), and the TOTEX efficiency incentive framework implicitly rewards DSOs whose network investments avoid carbon-intensive reinforcement. The D1–D4 avoided-cost values already capture part of this channel — VESG captures the residual societal benefit beyond network economics.

2.6   SFDR & Investor Reporting

Under SFDR (Regulation 2019/2088), Article 9 “dark green” funds must demonstrate a measurable sustainability objective. Quantified avoided emissions and social impact metrics are required for Principal Adverse Impact (PAI) indicators. VESG provides the underlying data for PAI Indicator 1 (GHG emissions), Indicator 3 (GHG intensity), and Indicator 6 (energy consumption intensity).

3   Formula Architecture

VESG is decomposed into three independently auditable pillars, each grounded in a distinct regulatory or market-practice channel:

VESG  =  E1carbon  +  E2greenium  +  E3social
where each Ei is a 30-year NPV at the project discount rate (WACCregulated for DSO perspective, WACCequity for investor perspective)
E1
Avoided Carbon Externality
Shadow value of CO2 emissions displaced by the BESS shifting load from fossil marginal plant to stored renewable energy. Monetised at a shadow carbon price.
E2
Green Financing Premium
NPV of the WACC reduction attributable to EU Taxonomy alignment — i.e., the “greenium” that lowers cost of capital for the BESS investor and, transitively, for the DSO’s avoided infrastructure spend.
E3
Social & Community Co-benefit
Quantified value of local air quality improvement (avoided NOx, PM2.5), energy resilience (avoided VOLL during outages), and employment/skills transfer during construction & O&M.
Σ
VESG (Total Uplift)
Sum of E1 + E2 + E3, reported as a 30-year NPV. Sits in the DSO table as the sixth row alongside D1–D5. Not added to VDSO proper — reported as a supplementary metric.

3.1   E1 — Avoided Carbon Externality

E1 = Σt=130  [ Qdisp,t × EFmarginal × Pcarbon,t ]  /  (1 + r)t
Qdisp,t = displaced fossil generation in year t (MWh)
EFmarginal = marginal emission factor (tCO2/MWh)
Pcarbon,t = shadow carbon price in year t (€/tCO2)
r = discount rate
VariableDefinitionDerivationCentral Estimate
Qdisp,t Annual energy displaced from fossil marginal plant by BESS arbitrage & balancing BESS capacity (MW) × equivalent full cycles/year × duration (h) × round-trip efficiency × (1 − curtailment factor). Derived from SSI-ENN dispatch model output. ~12,000–18,000 MWh/yr for a 10MW/40MWh system
EFmarginal Carbon intensity of displaced marginal plant (Southern Italy CCGT) ISPRA marginal factor or Terna dispatch data. JRC database cross-check. 0.38 tCO2/MWh
Pcarbon,t Shadow carbon price path (€/tCO2) Three scenarios: (a) EU ETS spot (floor, ~€75); (b) Mid-point €125; (c) EPA SCC (~€175, ceiling). Real escalation 2%/yr applied. €75–175/tCO2
r Discount rate Regulated WACC for DSO perspective; project WACC for investor perspective 5.2%
Illustrative E1 (central case): 15,000 MWh/yr × 0.38 tCO2/MWh × €125/tCO2 = €712,500/yr avoided carbon cost. 30-year NPV at 5.2% ≈ €10.6M. Range: €6.4M (ETS floor) – €14.8M (SCC ceiling).

3.2   E2 — Green Financing Premium

E2 = Σt=130  [ CAPEXoutstanding,t × ΔWACCgreen ]  /  (1 + r)t
CAPEXoutstanding,t = outstanding capital in year t (debt & equity amortisation schedule)
ΔWACCgreen = blended WACC reduction from Taxonomy alignment (bps)
VariableDefinitionDerivationCentral Estimate
ΔWACCgreen Blended WACC reduction attributable to EU Taxonomy alignment Debt greenium (3–15 bps, per Amundi/BNEF 2024 data) + equity CoE reduction (10–30 bps, per MSCI empirical studies). Weighted by D/E ratio. 15–25 bps
CAPEXoutstanding,t Capital base subject to financing cost Project finance amortisation schedule (typically 70:30 D/E, 18-year debt tenor) €7.0M year 1, declining
Illustrative E2: Average outstanding capital ~€4.0M over 30 years × 20 bps = €8,000/yr. 30-year NPV at 5.2% ≈ €0.12M. This is the smallest component — its value is primarily in signalling Taxonomy alignment rather than in absolute NPV contribution.

3.3   E3 — Social & Community Co-benefit

E3 = Σt=130  [ AQt + VOLLt + JOBt ]  /  (1 + r)t
AQt = avoided air pollution damage (€/yr) — NOx, SO2, PM2.5
VOLLt = avoided Value of Lost Load from resilience improvement (€/yr)
JOBt = local employment & skills transfer value (€/yr)
VariableDefinitionDerivationCentral Estimate
AQt Avoided health damage from displaced fossil plant pollutants Qdisp,t × pollutant emission factor (EEA) × damage cost per tonne (ExternE/NEEDS methodology, updated by EEA 2023). NOx ~€15,000/t, PM2.5 ~€40,000/t. ~€45,000–90,000/yr
VOLLt Value of avoided outage minutes due to BESS islanding / black-start capability Expected unserved energy (MWh) × VOLL (€/MWh). ACER/CEER estimate VOLL at €10,000–15,000/MWh for Italy (residential + commercial blend). ~€20,000–50,000/yr
JOBt Local employment multiplier during construction (2 yr) and O&M (28 yr) Construction: direct FTE × local wage premium. O&M: technician FTE × local multiplier (IRENA methodology). Typically front-loaded. ~€30,000–60,000/yr (avg)
Illustrative E3 (central case): (€67k + €35k + €45k)/yr = €147,000/yr. 30-year NPV at 5.2% ≈ €2.2M. Range: €1.4M (conservative) – €3.0M (high scenario).

4   Formula Validation Framework

Each component must pass four validation gates before inclusion in a reported VESG figure:

GateTestApplied ToMethod
V1 Regulatory Grounding All (E1, E2, E3) Each variable must trace to a published regulation, delegated act, or recognised methodology (EU Taxonomy, EU ETS, ISPRA, ACER, EEA ExternE). No purely academic or proprietary inputs.
V2 Additionality E1 primarily Displaced emissions must be additional — i.e., the BESS operation causes fossil displacement that would not occur without it. Validated by comparing dispatch-model counterfactual (no-BESS) to actual dispatch.
V3 No Double-Counting E1 vs D1–D4; E3 vs VCommunity E1 must not overlap with D2 (congestion relief already monetised as avoided curtailment). E3’s employment component must not double-count FTE already in VCommunity W3 (local employment). Cross-reference matrix required.
V4 Sensitivity & Bounds All Monte Carlo analysis (10,000 iterations per SSI-ENN standard) varying Pcarbon (±40%), EFmarginal (±15%), Qdisp (±20%), VOLL (±50%). Report P10/P50/P90 confidence intervals.
Double-counting watch: The most critical risk is between E1 (avoided carbon) and D2 (congestion relief). D2 values avoided DER curtailment in € — when BESS absorbs congestion, it also displaces fossil generation. To avoid overlap, E1 should use only the net incremental displaced MWh beyond what D2 already captures. In practice: E1 uses arbitrage + frequency response dispatch volumes; D2 uses congestion-specific dispatch volumes. The dispatch model must tag each MWh with its primary service.

6   Implementation Roadmap for v27+

6.1   Integration into the SSI-ENN Model

VESG should be computed as a parallel module within the Monte Carlo engine, using the same 10,000-iteration framework as VDSO. The dispatch model already produces Qdisp,t per iteration — the ESG module simply applies the E1/E2/E3 formulas to each iteration’s dispatch output and aggregates to P10/P50/P90.

6.2   Reporting Position

In the v1 report, VESG occupies the sixth row of the DSO value table (after D5 and before any totals). It should be:

  • •  Displayed as a separate line, not added to VDSO (to preserve the D1–D4 NPV integrity)
  • •  Tagged with a dashed border to visually distinguish it from the regulated D-components
  • •  Accompanied by a confidence interval (P10/P90) given the wide range of carbon price assumptions
  • •  Cross-referenced to this methodology note for audit trail

7   Governance & Audit Requirements

RequirementDetail
Data Vintage RuleAll inputs must use the most recent available vintage at the time of calculation. No input may be older than T−3 years from the report date. If ISPRA has not updated, use the JRC cross-check as primary.
Carbon Price LockPcarbon must be locked to a specific observation date (e.g., “EU ETS settlement 28 Feb 2026”) and disclosed. Forward curve assumptions must state the source and observation date.
Scenario DisclosureAll three Pcarbon scenarios (ETS floor, mid-point, SCC ceiling) must be reported. The central estimate may use the mid-point, but the floor and ceiling must be visible.
Additionality StatementEach report must include a signed statement that the avoided emissions are additional (dispatch counterfactual documented) and not claimed elsewhere (e.g., not sold as carbon credits or Guarantees of Origin).
Independent VerificationVESG should be subject to limited assurance (ISAE 3000) by a third-party ESG auditor, aligned with SFDR PAI reporting requirements.
Refresh CycleVESG must be recalculated annually using updated inputs. The recalculation date must be within 90 days of the annual ISPRA publication.
