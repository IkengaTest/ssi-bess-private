Methodology

How the BESS Valuation Platform scores and values substations — from raw data ingestion through resilience scoring to investment-grade financial modelling. The methodology is built on the SSI v4.0 open framework, extended with proprietary BESS sizing, value stacking, and real options pricing.

Breaking the Silos — Then Pricing Them

Traditional BESS siting relies on congestion data alone. The SSI-ENN platform starts where others stop: it fuses grid stress with socio-economic consequence, environmental exposure, and energy-transition readiness — then converts each dimension into a quantified revenue stream for the battery operator.

Foundation Layer

SSI v4.0 Composite Score

The Systemic System Infrastructure Index produces a composite resilience score R for each substation. Six weighted components — Congestion (C, 30%), Voltage Support (V, 10%), Intraday Variation (I, 25%), Frequency (E, 10%), Social Impact (S, 20%), and Transmission (T, 5%) — are normalised, weighted, and adjusted by five contextual modifiers (R2–R7) capturing transmission congestion, renewable penetration, load profile, network topology, and regulatory readiness.

R_base = 0.30·C + 0.10·V + 0.25·I + 0.10·E + 0.20·S + 0.05·T
R_final = R_base × R2 × R3 × R4 × R6 × R7

Valuation Layer

Three-Pillar Value Stack

Total BESS value at each substation is the sum of three distinct revenue/benefit pillars, each grounded in the substation's SSI component scores.

Total Value = V_DNO + V_Community + V_RO

V_DNO — Distribution Network Operator Value

Revenue from services sold to the grid operator: congestion relief, voltage regulation, capacity deferral, and resilience provision. Derived primarily from the C, V, and I components of the SSI score. Higher grid stress = higher DNO willingness to pay.

V_Community — Socio-Economic Value

Monetised societal benefit: CO₂ avoidance (€100/ton), energy independence improvement, and local economic development. Driven by the S (Social Impact) and E (Frequency/Economic) components. Captures the value that justifies public co-investment and regulatory support.

V_RO — Real Options Value

Flexibility premium from Monte Carlo simulation (10,000 iterations): the option value of being able to respond to future market, technology, and regulatory shifts. Accounts for price volatility, degradation uncertainty, and policy evolution.
